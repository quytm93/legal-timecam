Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 12, 2026

1. Introduction

TimeCam ("we," "our," or "us") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

Key Points: Your photos are processed locally on your device. We do not upload, store, or have access to your photos.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Subscription Information: If you purchase a subscription, payment processing is handled by Apple. We receive confirmation of your subscription status but not your payment details.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device Information: Device model, operating system version, unique device identifiers, and app version.
  • Usage Data: App interactions, features used, and crash reports to help us improve the app.
  • Analytics Data: Aggregated and anonymized usage statistics.

2.3 Information Collected with Your Permission

  • Camera Access: Required to take photos. Camera data is processed locally and not transmitted.
  • Photo Library Access: Required to save photos to your device. We do not access or upload existing photos.
  • Location Data: Optional. Used only to display location information on your timestamp overlays. Location data is processed locally and not stored on our servers.
  • App Tracking: With your consent, we may collect advertising identifiers to show personalized ads.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information to:

  • Provide and maintain the app's functionality
  • Process your subscription purchases
  • Improve and optimize the app's performance
  • Analyze usage patterns to enhance user experience
  • Display advertisements (with your consent for personalized ads)
  • Send important notices about changes to the app or policies
  • Detect and prevent technical issues or fraud

4. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may collect information:

4.1 Firebase (Google)

  • Firebase Analytics: Collects anonymized usage data
  • Firebase Crashlytics: Collects crash reports and diagnostic data
  • Firebase Remote Config: Delivers app configuration updates

For more information, see Firebase Privacy Policy.

4.2 Google AdMob

We display advertisements through Google AdMob. With your consent, personalized ads may be shown based on your interests.

For more information, see Google Privacy Policy.

4.3 Apple App Store

Subscription purchases and payments are processed by Apple through the App Store.

For more information, see Apple Privacy Policy.

5. Data Retention

We retain collected information for as long as necessary to provide our services and fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy. Analytics and crash data may be retained for up to 14 months for analysis purposes.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure.

7. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your information:

  • Access and Control: You can manage app permissions through your device settings.
  • Location Data: You can disable location services in your device settings.
  • Advertising Preferences: You can opt out of personalized advertising through your device settings (Settings > Privacy > Tracking).
  • Data Deletion: Contact us to request deletion of your data.

8. Children's Privacy

TimeCam is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn that we have collected such information, we will take steps to delete it.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have different data protection laws. By using the app, you consent to such transfers.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

12. California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information we collect and the right to request deletion of your data.

13. European Users (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including:

  • Right to access your personal data
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing

To exercise these rights, please contact us at neklasmicheal@gmail.com.
